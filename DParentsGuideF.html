💡 How to Support Your Child

  • Set consistent daily practice times — even 15–20 minutes makes a big difference.
  • Celebrate small wins to keep your child motivated and confident.
  • Use multisensory learning — trace letters in sand, use clay shapes, or sing letter sounds.
  • Read aloud together daily to improve phonological awareness.
  • Avoid timed pressure — learners benefit from extra processing time.
  • Encourage strength-based activities: art, music, storytelling, and building.

🥗 Brain-Boosting Nutrition

🍎 Daily Recommendations

  • Include omega-3 rich foods: salmon, walnuts, flaxseeds — great for focus and memory.
  • Ensure adequate iron intake — iron deficiency can affect attention and learning.
  • Limit sugary snacks before study sessions — they cause energy crashes.
  • Stay hydrated — even mild dehydration affects concentration.
  • Blueberries and leafy greens contain antioxidants that support brain health.

🏃 Weekly Activity Log

🤸 Recommended Activities

  • Cross-body exercises (e.g., touching right hand to left knee) — help with brain hemisphere coordination.
  • Yoga and mindfulness — reduce anxiety often associated with learning differences.
  • Swimming — builds rhythm and coordination beneficial for reading.
  • Juggling — improves visual tracking and focus.
  • Dance and music — strengthen phonological awareness through rhythm.
